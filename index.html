Fact finding

Questions to Ask Before Deciding

Use these prompts in manager interviews, role reviews, and classification file notes.

Business operation

  • What does the business primarily sell or deliver?
  • What generates most revenue and labour hours?
  • Are divisions operationally separate or merely supporting the main business?
  • How does the business describe itself commercially?

Role substance

  • What tasks are performed regularly?
  • What percentage of time is spent on each duty?
  • What qualifications, licences, training, or specialist skill are required?
  • Does the employee supervise, coordinate, or make operational decisions?

Judgment test

Miscellaneous Award or Award-Free?

The uploaded material warns against jumping straight from “no obvious award” to “award-free”. Use this checklist to slow that decision down.

Consider Miscellaneous Award where
  • No industry award clearly covers the employer and role.
  • No occupational award clearly covers the actual role or profession.
  • The employee is not clerical, professional, or managerial.
  • The work is operational, lower-skilled, junior, support-based, trade, advanced trade, or sub-professional.
  • The role fits within the Miscellaneous Award classification structure.
Consider award-free only where
  • Industry awards, occupational awards, and Miscellaneous Award have been assessed and excluded.
  • The employee is senior, managerial, highly specialised, or genuinely professional.
  • The role involves significant autonomy, strategic responsibility, or substantial independent judgment.
  • The work exceeds the type of classifications contemplated by modern awards generally.
  • The conclusion is based on duties and exclusions, not salary or job title.
